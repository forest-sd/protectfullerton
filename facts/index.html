The Facts

Making informed decisions starts with accurate information.

Know Your Government

Fullerton operates under a council-manager form of government, with five elected council members who set policy direction and a city manager who oversees daily operations. Understanding this structure is essential for effective civic participation. Council meetings are open to the public and provide opportunities for residents to comment on agenda items and raise concerns. Beyond council meetings, numerous boards and commissions advise the city on planning, parks, transportation, and other issues — all accepting volunteer members from the community.

Your Voice Matters

Local government decisions directly affect your daily life — from road maintenance and public safety to zoning, parks, and water rates. Yet local elections consistently see lower turnout than state and national races. When residents don't participate, decisions get made by a small group that may not represent the broader community's interests. Every voice, every vote, every public comment matters.
